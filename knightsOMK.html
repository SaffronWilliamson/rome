-Knights of Malta Keyhole-

History

The Knights of Malta Keyhole is one of Rome’s most intriguing hidden gems, offering a perfectly framed view of St. Peter’s Basilica through a small keyhole in a door on the Aventine Hill. The keyhole belongs to the Priory of the Knights of Malta, a sovereign order with medieval origins dating back to the 11th century. The Grand Priory of Rome has been the headquarters of the Knights of Malta, a Catholic military and charitable order, for centuries. Though the priory itself is not open to the public, visitors gather to peek through the keyhole, where an astonishing optical illusion reveals a perfectly aligned pathway of hedges leading to St. Peter’s Dome.

Architectural & Symbolic Significance

The Keyhole View

  • The keyhole’s alignment with St. Peter’s Basilica is no coincidence—it is believed to have been deliberately designed to create this stunning perspective.
  • The view frames three nations at once: Italy (the Aventine Hill), Vatican City (St. Peter’s Basilica), and Sovereign Order of Malta (the priory itself, which holds extraterritorial status).

The Knights of Malta Priory

  • The priory, though private, is known for its stunning gardens and rich history.
  • The Knights of Malta are one of the oldest chivalric orders, once serving as protectors of Christian pilgrims.
  • Their sovereign status means the priory is not officially part of Italy—it is a tiny independent entity similar to Vatican City.

The Aventine Hill & Nearby Landmarks

  • The Aventine Hill is one of the seven hills of Rome, known for its quiet atmosphere, stunning views, and ancient history.
  • Nearby is the Orange Garden (Giardino degli Aranci), offering panoramic views of the city.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • A Secret Perspective – Many visitors stumble upon the keyhole by accident, unaware of its perfectly framed view of St. Peter’s Basilica.
  • An Ancient Order with Modern Influence – The Knights of Malta still exist today, operating as a sovereign entity engaged in humanitarian aid and diplomatic relations.
  • A Romantic Spot – The keyhole is often visited by couples, as it’s considered one of the most mysterious and romantic viewpoints in Rome.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Sunset or early morning for the most magical lighting and fewer crowds.

Nearby Attractions

  • Orange Garden (Giardino degli Aranci) – A stunning park with breathtaking city views.
  • Santa Sabina Basilica – A 5th-century church known for its ancient wooden doors and peaceful cloister.
  • Circus Maximus – The ancient Roman chariot racing stadium is just downhill from the Aventine Hill.
