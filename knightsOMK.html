-Knights of Malta Keyhole-

History

The Knights of Malta Keyhole is part of the Aventine Hill, an area rich in history and home to various ancient and medieval sites. The Sovereign Military Order of Malta, a Catholic lay religious order, has occupied the Palazzo dei Cavalieri di Malta (Palace of the Knights of Malta) for centuries. The keyhole itself was intentionally aligned to frame the view of St. Peter’s Basilica when looking through it. This alignment is a stunning example of the importance of perspective and hidden details within Rome’s architecture. The exact origin of the keyhole’s alignment is unclear, but it is believed to have been created during the 18th century, as part of renovations to the palace.

The Keyhole View

Looking through the Knights of Malta Keyhole, you see a striking and perfectly symmetrical view of St. Peter’s Basilica in the distance. The perspective is fascinating: a lush green path leading to the gates of the Knights of Malta’s headquarters, followed by a glimpse of the basilica’s iconic dome framed by a tunnel of greenery. The view has earned the keyhole its reputation as one of the most beautiful and unexpected sights in Rome.

Why It’s Special

What makes the Knights of Malta Keyhole so special is the way it embodies the layered history of Rome. From ancient times to the present, the city has been built with intentionality, with many hidden gems like this one that reveal themselves to the discerning eye. The perspective of St. Peter’s Basilica, visible through a simple keyhole, speaks to the city’s historical, religious, and architectural significance.

Visiting the Knights of Malta Keyhole

  • Location: Piazza dei Cavalieri di Malta, Aventine Hill, Rome.
  • Opening Hours: The keyhole is visible at all times, but the best experience comes during daylight hours.
  • Admission: Free to visit.
  • Best Time to Visit: Visit during the early morning or late afternoon when the light and shadows create a magical atmosphere around the view.

Nearby Attractions

  • Aventine Hill – The area surrounding the keyhole is a peaceful, picturesque neighborhood, home to the Orange Garden (Giardino degli Aranci), which offers stunning views of Rome.
  • Basilica of Santa Sabina – A beautiful early Christian church located on Aventine Hill, known for its ancient doors and serene atmosphere.
  • The Keyhole of the Priory of the Knights of Malta is a perfect addition to a stroll around the Aventine Hill, offering a glimpse into Rome’s rich history.

Fun Facts & Curiosities

  • The keyhole’s unique alignment with St. Peter’s Basilica is not only a beautiful sight, but it also serves as a metaphor for the connection between the Sovereign Military Order of Malta and the Catholic Church.
  • The Aventine Hill itself has a rich history as one of the Seven Hills of Rome, and today it remains one of the city’s most tranquil and picturesque neighborhoods.
  • Many visitors enjoy recreating the famous photo of St. Peter’s Dome through the keyhole, making it one of the most photographed and shared spots in Rome.
