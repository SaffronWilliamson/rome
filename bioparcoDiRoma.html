-Bioparco di Roma-

History

Founded in 1911, Bioparco di Roma was originally designed as a traditional zoological garden, meant to showcase exotic animals for public enjoyment. Over the decades, it evolved into a modern conservation-focused park, dedicated to protecting endangered species and promoting ecological awareness. Today, Bioparco prioritizes animal welfare and environmental education while offering visitors an engaging and immersive experience.

Animal Highlights & Attractions

Big Cats

  • Lions: The park houses a group of majestic African lions, one of the most popular attractions.
  • Tigers: Visitors can admire the rare Sumatran tiger, an endangered species.
  • Leopards: Agile and elusive, these magnificent creatures are a favorite among visitors.

Great Apes & Primates

  • Chimpanzees & Orangutans: Bioparco is home to several great apes, emphasizing conservation efforts for these intelligent species.
  • Lemurs: These playful primates from Madagascar are always a hit, especially with children.

Elephants & Giraffes

  • Asian Elephants: The zoo has a dedicated habitat for these gentle giants.
  • Giraffes: Towering above the landscape, these elegant creatures are a highlight of the park.

Reptile House

  • Featuring crocodiles, snakes, and turtles, this section provides insight into some of the world’s most fascinating cold-blooded creatures.

Aquatic & Exotic Birds

  • A diverse collection of colourful birds, including flamingos, parrots, and eagles, adds to the vibrant atmosphere.

Conservation & Education Efforts

  • Endangered Species Protection: The Bioparco participates in international breeding programs to help preserve threatened species.
  • Animal Welfare Focus: Modern enclosures replicate natural habitats to ensure the animals' well-being.
  • Educational Workshops: Regular events, guided tours, and interactive exhibits teach visitors about wildlife conservation and biodiversity.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • An Elephant’s Tale: One of the zoo’s most famous past residents was Fritz the Elephant, who lived in Bioparco for decades and became a beloved symbol of the park.
  • Rome’s Hidden Safari: Many visitors are surprised to find such a diverse collection of animals right in the heart of the city.
  • A Historic Setting: Unlike many modern zoos, Bioparco retains its original early 20th-century architecture, adding a unique charm to the experience.
  • One of Europe’s Oldest Zoos: Founded over 110 years ago, Bioparco is one of the longest-running zoological parks in the world.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Morning visits allow visitors to see the animals when they are most active.
  • Spring and autumn offer the best weather for exploring the park.

Family-Friendly Activities

  • Interactive experiences such as animal feeding sessions and educational talks.
  • A small train ride that provides a fun tour of the park.

Nearby Attractions

  • Galleria Borghese – A world-famous art museum within Villa Borghese.
  • Pincio Terrace – A scenic viewpoint overlooking Piazza del Popolo.
  • Piazza di Siena – A historic equestrian arena in the park.
