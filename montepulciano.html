-Montepulciano-

History

Montepulciano is a medieval hilltop town in Tuscany, famous for its wine, Renaissance architecture, and breathtaking views. Founded by the Etruscans in the 4th century BC, it later flourished under the Medici family during the Renaissance. Throughout history, Montepulciano was a strategic stronghold, contested between Florence and Siena due to its prime location. Today, it is known for its charming streets, historic palaces, and world-renowned Vino Nobile di Montepulciano wine.

Architectural & Cultural Marvels

Piazza Grande

  • The heart of Montepulciano, surrounded by Renaissance palaces and historic buildings.
  • Home to the Palazzo Comunale, which resembles Florence’s Palazzo Vecchio and offers a panoramic tower climb.
  • The square is often used for festivals, markets, and film locations, including scenes from Twilight: New Moon.

Monteulciano Cathedral (Duomo di Montepulciano)

  • A 16th-century cathedral with a strikingly simple façade and grand interior.
  • Inside, visitors can see Taddeo di Bartolo’s famous Assumption of the Virgin.

Underground Wine Cellars

  • Montepulciano is built over a network of ancient cellars, some dating back to Etruscan times.
  • Many wineries offer tours and tastings, allowing visitors to experience the town’s winemaking tradition firsthand.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • The Town of Wine – Montepulciano is famous for Vino Nobile di Montepulciano, one of Italy’s oldest and most prestigious wines.
  • A Filmmaker’s Dream – The town’s medieval streets and scenic landscapes have been featured in several films, including Twilight: New Moon.
  • Etruscan Origins – Some of Montepulciano’s wine cellars and tunnels date back over 2,000 years to the Etruscan civilization.

Must-Try Foods & Wine

  • Vino Nobile di Montepulciano – A rich, full-bodied red wine, best enjoyed with local dishes.
  • Pici Pasta – A hand-rolled, thick pasta often served with wild boar ragù or pecorino cheese.
  • Cantucci & Vin Santo – Almond biscotti paired with sweet dessert wine.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring and fall for the best weather and fewer crowds.
  • Visit in August for the Bravio delle Botti, a traditional barrel-rolling race through the streets.

Nearby Attractions

  • Pienza – A UNESCO-listed town known for its Renaissance architecture and pecorino cheese.
  • Val d'Orcia – A stunning countryside region with rolling hills, cypress trees, and picturesque villages.
  • Bagno Vignoni – A historic thermal spa town with natural hot springs.
