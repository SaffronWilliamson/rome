-Mouth of Truth-

History

The Mouth of Truth (Bocca della Verità) is one of Rome’s most famous and mysterious landmarks. This massive marble mask, depicting a bearded face, dates back to the 1st century AD and is believed to have been part of an ancient Roman fountain or a drain cover. Its true purpose remains unknown, but over time, it became associated with a medieval legend that claims it can detect liars. The superstition states that if someone puts their hand inside the mouth and tells a lie, their hand will be bitten off! Today, the Bocca della Verità is located inside the portico of the Church of Santa Maria in Cosmedin, where thousands of visitors test their honesty each year.

Architectural & Symbolic Significance

The Marble Mask

  • Carved from Pavonazzo marble, the circular mask measures 1.75 meters (5.7 feet) in diameter.
  • The face is often believed to represent a river god, Jupiter, or even the oracle of Faunus, linked to ancient Roman mythology.

Santa Maria in Cosmedin

  • The church housing the Mouth of Truth is a beautiful medieval basilica known for its Cosmatesque floor, bell tower, and ancient relics.
  • It is famous for containing the skull of Saint Valentine, making it a popular site for couples.

The Medieval Lie Detector Legend

  • The Bocca della Verità was first mentioned in medieval texts, where it was believed to test a person’s truthfulness.
  • In the Middle Ages, suspected liars, cheaters, and adulterers were forced to put their hands inside to prove their innocence.
  • Some versions of the legend say judges used hidden mechanisms to create the illusion of punishment.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • Hollywood Fame – The Mouth of Truth became world-famous after appearing in the 1953 movie Roman Holiday, where Audrey Hepburn and Gregory Peck took part in the hand-in-the-mouth tradition.
  • Real-life Fear – Gregory Peck pretended to lose his hand in the movie scene, causing a genuine shocked reaction from Audrey Hepburn.
  • Secret Pagan Rituals? – Some historians believe the Mouth of Truth was part of pagan rituals, where Romans made oaths to the gods.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Early morning or late afternoon to avoid long lines.
  • Free to visit, but a small donation to the church is encouraged.

Nearby Attractions

  • The Roman Forum – Just a 5-minute walk away, offering a glimpse into ancient Rome.
  • Piazza della Bocca della Verità – Home to ancient temples like the Temple of Hercules.
  • Tiber Island – A short walk across the river, known for its scenic views and history.
