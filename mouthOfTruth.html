-Mouth of Truth-

History

The Mouth of Truth is a large, circular marble relief measuring about 1.75 meters (5 feet 9 inches) in diameter. Dating back to the 1st century AD, it is believed to have originally been a manhole cover or part of an ancient Roman fountain. The face carved into the stone is thought to represent a river god, such as Oceanus or Tiberinus, the deity of the Tiber River. The stone gained legendary status in the Middle Ages when it became associated with the belief that it could reveal dishonesty. According to tradition, anyone who placed their hand inside the mouth and told a lie would have their hand bitten off. This superstition has made the Bocca della Verità one of Rome’s most iconic and photographed attractions.

The Legend of the Mouth of Truth

One of the most famous stories associated with the Bocca della Verità involves a woman accused of adultery. To prove her innocence, she devised a clever trick. Before placing her hand in the mouth, she had her lover-disguised as a stranger-kiss her in front of the crowd. She then swore she had only ever been touched by her husband and "this man" (referring to her lover). The Mouth of Truth did not punish her, as she technically told the truth, showcasing the ingenuity of ancient Romans.

Pop Culture & Cinema

  • The Mouth of Truth gained international fame in the 1953 film Roman Holiday, starring Audrey Hepburn and Gregory Peck. In a playful scene, Peck's character pretends his hand has been bitten off, causing Hepburn’s character to scream in shock.
  • The site has been featured in numerous travel documentaries, books, and films, cementing its status as one of Rome’s most recognizable landmarks.

Visiting the Mouth of Truth

  • Location: Piazza della Bocca della Verità, near the Tiber Island and the Forum Boarium.
  • Opening Hours: Generally open daily, but hours may vary depending on the Church of Santa Maria in Cosmedin’s schedule.
  • Admission: Visiting is free, but a small donation is encouraged.
  • Best Time to Visit: Early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid long lines.

Nearby Attractions

  • Santa Maria in Cosmedin – The medieval church housing the Bocca della Verità, famous for its stunning Cosmatesque floor and relics of Saint Valentine.
  • Temple of Hercules Victor – An ancient round temple dating back to the 2nd century BC, one of the oldest surviving marble buildings in Rome.
  • Tiber Island – A picturesque island in the Tiber River, known for its historic hospital and scenic walkways.

Fun Facts & Curiosities

  • Some historians believe the Mouth of Truth was once part of an ancient Roman drain cover, possibly used for sacrificial ceremonies.
  • In medieval times, it was thought that priests used the legend to scare people into honesty, reinforcing moral behavior.
  • Today, visitors love to recreate the famous hand-in-the-mouth scene from Roman Holiday, making it one of the most photographed spots in Rome.
