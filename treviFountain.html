-Trevi Fountain-

History

The Trevi Fountain was designed by Nicola Salvi in 1732 and completed by Giuseppe Pannini in 1762. It was commissioned by Pope Clement XII as part of a renovation project to improve the city’s water supply. The fountain marks the endpoint of the Aqua Virgo, an ancient Roman aqueduct constructed in 19 BC, which still supplies water to the fountain today. The name “Trevi” is believed to come from the Latin “Trivium,” referring to the three roads that converge at the site.

Architectural Highlights

The Central Figure: Oceanus

  • At the center of the fountain stands Oceanus, the god of all water, riding a shell-shaped chariot pulled by two sea horses.
  • One horse is calm, while the other is wild, symbolizing the different moods of the sea.

The Side Statues

  • To the left of Oceanus is a statue representing Abundance, holding a horn of plenty.
  • To the right is Salubrity, symbolising health and purity, with a snake drinking from a cup.
  • Above these figures, bas-reliefs depict Agrippa and a young girl (the “Virgin”) who, according to legend, guided Roman engineers to the source of the Aqua Virgo.

The Grand Cascade

  • Water flows over artificial rock formations into a large basin below, creating a mesmerising waterfall effect.
  • The sound of rushing water enhances the magical atmosphere, making it one of the most enchanting sights in Rome.

Legends & Traditions

  • Throwing a Coin: According to tradition, tossing a coin over your left shoulder into the fountain ensures a return to Rome. Throwing two coins means you’ll find love, and three means marriage! Each year, around €1.5 million in coins are collected and donated to charity.
  • Movie Fame: The fountain gained worldwide fame thanks to its appearance in Federico Fellini’s “La Dolce Vita” (1960), in which Anita Ekberg wades through its waters.
  • Papal Influence: Pope Urban VIII originally wanted the fountain to be much grander, but his vision was never realised due to funding issues.

Visiting Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late at night to avoid large crowds.
  • Photo Tips: The best pictures can be taken from the steps in front of the fountain, but patience is required due to crowds.
  • No Wading Allowed: Unlike in the movies, stepping into the fountain is strictly prohibited and can result in hefty fines!
