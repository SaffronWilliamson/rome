-Villa Borghese-

History

Villa Borghese is Rome’s most famous and expansive park, often called the “green heart” of the city. Originally a private vineyard, it was transformed in 1605 by Cardinal Scipione Borghese, the nephew of Pope Paul V, into a grand estate with landscaped gardens and a magnificent villa to house his impressive art collection. In 1903, the Italian government acquired Villa Borghese, opening it as a public park, making it one of the most beloved green spaces in Rome. Today, it is home to museums, fountains, lakes, and scenic walkways, offering a perfect blend of nature, art, and history.

Architectural & Natural Marvels

Galleria Borghese

  • A world-renowned museum featuring masterpieces by Caravaggio, Raphael, Bernini, and Titian.
  • Highlights include Bernini’s “Apollo and Daphne” and Caravaggio’s “Boy with a Basket of Fruit”.

Temple of Aesculapius

  • A picturesque neoclassical temple located on an artificial lake.
  • One of the most photographed spots in the park, offering boat rentals for a romantic ride.

Piazza di Siena

  • A historic oval-shaped area used for equestrian events, including the famous Piazza di Siena Horse Show.

Bioparco di Roma (Rome’s Zoo)

  • Established in 1911, this zoological park is home to over 1,000 animals from around the world.
  • A great attraction for families visiting Rome.

Pincian Terrace (Terrazza del Pincio)

  • One of the best panoramic viewpoints in Rome, offering breathtaking views over Piazza del Popolo and the city skyline.
  • A popular spot for sunset photography and romantic walks.

Silvano Toti Globe Theatre

  • A replica of Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre in London, hosting performances of classic plays.

Casino della Meridiana & Other Hidden Gems

  • Scattered throughout the park are smaller museums, pavilions, and historical structures worth discovering.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • The Ghost of Villa Borghese: Some legends claim that the spirits of past Borghese family members still roam the park at night.
  • The Rivalry Between Bernini & Borromini: The villa features sculptures by Gian Lorenzo Bernini, a favorite of Cardinal Scipione Borghese, who was a great rival of architect Francesco Borromini.
  • A Park of Hidden Symbols: Many statues and fountains in Villa Borghese have hidden allegorical meanings related to ancient mythology and Rome’s noble families.
  • Rome’s First Public Cinema – The Casa del Cinema, inside the park, is dedicated to Italian and international films, offering screenings and exhibitions.
  • The Park’s Secret Underground Passages – Villa Borghese is said to have hidden underground tunnels, possibly used by the Borghese family to escape during sieges.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Early morning or late afternoon for a peaceful experience.
  • Spring and autumn offer the most beautiful natural scenery.

How to Explore

  • Rent a bike or segway to cover more ground efficiently.
  • Take a leisurely stroll and enjoy the sculptures, fountains, and hidden corners.

Nearby Attractions

  • Piazza del Popolo – A short walk from the Pincio Terrace with stunning architecture.
  • Via Veneto – Famous for its elegant cafés and La Dolce Vita history.
  • Spanish Steps – Easily accessible from the park, ideal for a combined visit.
