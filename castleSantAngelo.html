-Castle Sant'Angelo-

History

Castel Sant’Angelo, also known as the Mausoleum of Hadrian, is one of Rome’s most fascinating and historically rich landmarks. It was originally built in 135–139 AD as a mausoleum for Emperor Hadrian and his family. Over the centuries, it was transformed into a fortress, papal residence, prison, and museum. During the Middle Ages, the castle became a military stronghold due to its strategic location near the Vatican. In the 6th century, it was renamed Castel Sant’Angelo after a legend in which Archangel Michael appeared atop the structure, sheathing his sword as a sign of the end of a deadly plague in Rome. The castle is also connected to St. Peter’s Basilica via a secret corridor called Passetto di Borgo, which popes used as an escape route during sieges.

Architectural Marvels

The Statue of Archangel Michael

  • The castle is named after Archangel Michael, who, according to legend, appeared above the fortress in 590 AD to signal the end of a deadly plague in Rome.
  • A grand bronze statue of the archangel stands at the top, replacing earlier versions over the centuries.

The Spiral Ramp and Imperial Tombs

  • Inside the castle, visitors can explore the original spiral ramp, designed to lead funeral processions to Hadrian’s tomb.
  • Though much of the ancient mausoleum has been repurposed, remnants of the imperial burial chambers can still be seen.

The Papal Apartments

  • Lavishly decorated with Renaissance frescoes, the papal apartments were used by popes as both a residence and a place of refuge.
  • Some rooms still feature stunning artworks and intricate ceilings that showcase the wealth and influence of the Catholic Church.

The Courtyard and Prison Cells

  • The fortress was infamous for its dark prison cells, where high-profile prisoners were held, often under harsh conditions.
  • Executions were also carried out in the castle, reinforcing its reputation as a place of both power and punishment.

Fun Facts & Legends

  • The Passetto di Borgo: This secret corridor was famously used by Pope Clement VII to escape during the Sack of Rome (1527).
  • A Papal Fortress – Several popes lived in Castel Sant’Angelo, fortifying it and adding luxurious Renaissance-style apartments.
  • Treasure Vault: Castel Sant’Angelo was rumored to contain hidden treasures, as it was used as a papal treasury for many years.
  • Prison & Execution Site – The castle served as a prison for high-profile figures, including Giordano Bruno and Benvenuto Cellini. Some executions also took place within its walls.

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.
  • Sunset visits are stunning, offering panoramic views of Rome from the terrace.

Nearby Attractions

  • St. Peter’s Basilica – A short walk across the Passetto di Borgo.
  • Ponte Sant’Angelo – The beautiful bridge lined with Bernini-designed angel statues.
  • Piazza Navona & Pantheon – Both within a 15-minute walk.
